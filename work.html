What we fund

UK Research and Innovation (UKRI) is at the heart of driving groundbreaking discoveries and transformative innovations. With thousands of projects funded across diverse fields—from cutting-edge science and technology to arts and humanities—we have allocated billions of pounds to support world-class research and innovation. These investments empower talented individuals, foster collaboration, and tackle global challenges, ensuring the UK remains a leader in research and innovation. Explore the exciting projects we fund and the impact they are making on society, the economy, and the environment.

National Facility for Ultra-High Field (11.7T) Human MRI Scanning

This groundbreaking facility houses the UK's first 11.7 Tesla MRI scanner, providing unprecedented image resolution and detail of the human brain.

The ultra-high field strength enables researchers to:

  • Visualize brain structures at near-microscopic level
  • Study neurological conditions with unprecedented detail
  • Develop new diagnostic techniques for early disease detection
Funding: £10.8 million
Duration: 2023-2028
Partners: University of Oxford, Imperial College London
AutoCHERI: Enhancing Automotive Cybersecurity

The AutoCHERI project implements CHERI (Capability Hardware Enhanced RISC Instructions) technology to significantly improve vehicle cybersecurity.

Key benefits include:

  • Protection against memory-based cyber attacks
  • Compartmentalization of critical vehicle systems
  • Enhanced protection for connected vehicle communications
Funding: £3.5 million
Duration: 2022-2025
Partners: University of Cambridge, Jaguar Land Rover
OxDrive: Electrifying Agricultural Vehicles

OxDrive is developing high-torque electric drivelines specifically designed for agricultural machinery, helping to decarbonize the farming sector.

Key innovations include:

  • High-density battery packs optimized for agricultural use
  • Motors capable of delivering sustained high torque
  • Field-serviceable components designed for harsh environments
Funding: £4.8 million
Duration: 2023-2026
Partners: Oxford University, John Deere, National Farmers Union
ANB Sensors: Advancing Ocean Acidity Monitoring

ANB Sensors is developing revolutionary calibration-free pH sensors for long-term ocean monitoring, critical for understanding the impacts of climate change on marine ecosystems.

Key achievements:

  • Created sensors that maintain accuracy for up to 12 months without recalibration
  • Developed miniaturized sensors compatible with autonomous underwater vehicles
  • Established a global network of monitoring stations tracking ocean acidification
Funding: £2.9 million
Duration: 2021-2025
Partners: University of Southampton, National Oceanography Centre, Plymouth Marine Laboratory
DARE UK: Enhancing Data Research Infrastructure

DARE UK (Data and Analytics Research Environments UK) is building a cutting-edge, privacy-preserving data research infrastructure to enable groundbreaking research while maintaining public trust.

Key developments:

  • Creating federated data systems that allow analysis without moving sensitive data
  • Implementing privacy-enhancing technologies to protect individual identities
  • Building cloud-based research environments with enhanced security capabilities
Funding: £8.3 million
Duration: 2022-2027
Partners: Health Data Research UK, Administrative Data Research UK
ARIA: The UK's 'Moonshot' Research Agency

The Advanced Research & Invention Agency (ARIA) represents the UK's commitment to high-risk, high-reward scientific research that could fundamentally transform our world.

ARIA focuses on:

  • Breakthrough technologies that address major societal challenges
  • Research projects too ambitious or radical for traditional funding mechanisms
  • Supporting talented scientists with minimal bureaucracy and maximum freedom
Funding: £800 million
Duration: 2022-2027
Structure: Independent agency with close ties to UKRI and university partnerships
